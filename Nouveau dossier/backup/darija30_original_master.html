What is Moroccan Darija?

If you've tried learning Arabic before visiting Morocco, you might be in for a surprise. Morocco has two main languages: Modern Standard Arabic (MSA) for writing/news, and Moroccan Darija for living.

The Difference

Imagine going to London and speaking Shakespearian English. People will understand you, but they'll look at you funny. That's what speaking MSA in a Moroccan market feels like.

Why is it different?

Darija is a beautiful blend. It's built on a foundation of Arabic, heavily influenced by the indigenous Amazigh (Berber) languages, and sprinkled with French and Spanish words due to history.

  • Cheese: Formage (French)
  • Week: Simana (Spanish)
  • Carrot: Khizou (Amazigh)

Don't waste time on standard Arabic if your goal is to travel, do business, or connect with Moroccan family. Learn Darija.

"Black coffee, please."

"3afak" is the magic word for "please". You can put it at the end of almost any request. "Ka7la" means black.
