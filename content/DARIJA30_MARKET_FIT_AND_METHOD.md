# Darija30 Phase D17 — Market Fit + Differentiation Decisions

## Final audience
Darija30 should not say “for Americans only.” The product is for **English speakers visiting Morocco**: travelers, first-time visitors, expats, digital nomads, and anyone who learns through English and does not want Arabic alphabet first.

## Competitive position
Darija30 is not a grammar course, not an academic Darija school, and not a long phrasebook. It is a **Moroccan situation trainer**.

The promise is practical:

- Learn what you will actually say.
- Hear what a Moroccan may say back.
- Practice one phrase per page.
- Use Friendly Latin Darija first.
- Keep Arabic script optional on lesson pages.
- Do not show Arabic script in micro videos.

## Lesson model
Core paid product:

- 30 lessons
- 5 core phrase pages per lesson
- 150 essential phrase pages total

Each page teaches one core phrase only. This protects simplicity and makes the product feel fast, approachable, and different from competitors.

## Dialogue video model
The page teaches the **Core Phrase**. The video shows the **real situation**.

Examples:

Taxi:
- Driver: `fin bghiti tmshi?` — Where do you want to go?
- Traveler/Core: `bghit nmshi l-otel` — I want to go to the hotel.

Market:
- Traveler/Core: `bsh-hal hada?` — How much is this?
- Seller: `khamsin drham` — 50 dirhams.

## Bonus Phrases
Bonus Phrases are optional. They should not replace the 5 core phrases or make the lesson feel heavy.

Use them after the learner completes the 5 core phrases:

- 150 core phrases = main product promise
- Bonus phrases = extra confidence
