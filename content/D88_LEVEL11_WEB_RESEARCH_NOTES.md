# D88 Level 11 Web Research Notes — Native Listening / WhatsApp / Media

## Scope
Level 11 focuses on real Moroccan digital communication: Latin Darija/Arabizi with numbers, WhatsApp short text, voice notes, phone-call repair phrases, fast street speech, short video/comment comprehension, tone/jokes, regional variation, delayed replies, misunderstanding repair, and turning messages into real-life actions.

## Research signals used
- Moroccan Arabic transcription references explain that Darija is commonly written in Latin characters in informal digital contexts, and that numbers such as 3 and 7 are used to represent Arabic sounds that do not exist in Latin letters.
- Arabizi/Arabic chat alphabet references confirm that Latin letters plus Western numerals are widely used for informal Arabic dialect communication online and by phone.
- Phone-call Darija references emphasize real phrases for unclear audio and unstable calls, including asking to repeat, saying the voice is low, checking if the other person is still with you, and mentioning the line being cut.
- Useful expression references confirm learner repair phrases such as “repeat please”, “I do not understand”, “no problem”, “help me please”, and short yes/no/thank-you responses.
- Recent Darija NLP research reinforces that Moroccan Arabic is a low-resource dialect with strong practical need for high-quality, reviewed Darija data; therefore these sentences remain draft until native Moroccan QA.

## Content policy
All Level 11 phrases are draft learning material. Native Moroccan QA is required before audio recording, public release, or media generation. Level 11 intentionally trains comprehension and repair strategies, not perfect word-for-word translation.
