# Darija30 — Phase D5 Audio Recording Script

## Recording philosophy

Darija30 is audio-first. The learner should hear a Moroccan phrase as a usable sound pattern before thinking about Arabic letters or grammar.

Record each phrase in two files:

1. `normal` — natural Moroccan speed, clear but not robotic.
2. `slow` — slow, phrase-by-phrase, still natural, not exaggerated.

Recommended voice direction:

- Warm, friendly, Moroccan, not formal news Arabic.
- No classroom tone.
- Leave 0.3 seconds silence before and after each clip.
- Do not add music.
- Same pronunciation across all clips.
- Export as MP3, 128kbps or higher.

## Friendly Latin policy

The website displays Friendly Latin Darija first. Moroccan chat spelling with numbers is hidden in details.

Examples:

- `aafak`, not `3afak` as the primary spelling.
- `qahwa`, not `9ahwa` as the primary spelling.
- `bsh-hal`, not `bsh7al` as the primary spelling.

## Quality check before shipping

- Every phrase must have a normal and slow file.
- File names must exactly match `lessons-data.js`.
- Listen on mobile speaker and headphones.
- Make sure slow audio is not too slow or unnatural.
- Confirm “aafak”, “qahwa”, “bsh-hal” are understandable for English speakers.
