## Lesson index

### Day 01 — Taxi Survival
- Module: 
- Situation: You just arrived in Morocco and need to move around by taxi without panic.

1. **I want to go to the hotel.** → `bghit nmshi l-otel`
2. **Stop here, please.** → `weqef hna aafak`
3. **Go straight, please.** → `sir nishan aafak`
4. **Turn right, please.** → `dor l-limn aafak`
5. **Here is good.** → `hna mezian`

### Day 02 — Cafe Culture
- Module: 
- Situation: You are in a Moroccan café and want to order clearly without needing Arabic letters.

1. **Black coffee, please.** → `qahwa kahla aafak`
2. **Mint tea, please.** → `atay b naanaa aafak`
3. **Without sugar, please.** → `bla sokar aafak`
4. **Water, please.** → `l-ma aafak`
5. **The bill, please.** → `l-hssab aafak`

### Day 03 — Market Price
- Module: 
- Situation: You are in a Moroccan market or small shop and need to ask prices without sounding formal.

1. **How much is this?** → `bsh-hal hada?`
2. **Too expensive.** → `ghali bzaf`
3. **Lower it a little, please.** → `nqess shwiya aafak`
4. **What is the last price?** → `shno akher taman?`
5. **I want to buy this.** → `bghit nshri hada`

### Day 04 — First Moroccan Greetings
- Module: First Contact & Confidence
- Situation: Walk into a shop, riad, taxi, or café and open the interaction warmly.

1. **Hello.** → `salam`
2. **How are you?** → `labas?`
3. **I am good, thank God.** → `bikhir, lhamdullah`
4. **Thank you.** → `shukran`
5. **Goodbye.** → `bslama`

### Day 05 — Be Polite Without Sounding Formal
- Module: First Contact & Confidence
- Situation: Use small polite words that make you sound respectful, not stiff.

1. **Please.** → `aafak`
2. **Excuse me / sorry.** → `smh liya`
3. **No problem.** → `maashi mushkil`
4. **Okay.** → `wakha`
5. **No, thank you.** → `la shukran`

### Day 06 — Introduce Yourself

### Day 07 — I Don’t Understand
- Module: Move Around Morocco
- Situation: Stay calm when you miss what someone said.

1. **I did not understand.** → `ma fhemtsh`

3. **Repeat, please.** → `aawed aafak`
4. **Do you speak English?** → `wash kat-hder English?`
5. **What did you say?** → `shnu glti?`
