# D83 — Level 6 Web Research Notes

Level: **6 — Friends & Family / الأصدقاء والعائلة**

## Research direction
Level 6 is not a tourist survival level. It teaches the learner how to behave and speak inside Moroccan social relationships: entering a home, greeting several people, asking about family, accepting tea/food, speaking with elders and children, handling photos, invitations, gifts, jokes, boundaries, WhatsApp coordination, and warm goodbyes.

## Web-aligned references used for content direction
- Moroccan hospitality and guest etiquette: greetings, `tfadal`, `marhba bik`, guest behavior, and the importance of entering warmly.
- Family gatherings: greeting everyone, asking about wellbeing, welcoming phrases, and individual greetings.
- Moroccan tea and food etiquette: tea as hospitality, accepting at least a small glass, thanking hosts, and respecting right-hand/shared-meal etiquette.
- Travel etiquette: bringing a small gift when invited, using greetings, thanking hosts, and respecting family/private topics.
- Peace Corps / Darija learning references: competency-based language learning and practical social/family vocabulary.

## Darija30 differentiation
This level is not a vocabulary list of family members. Each phrase is written as a **social micro-scene**:

`place/event → learner goal → Darija sentence → likely Moroccan reply → culture note → image/video seed`

## QA warning
All 150 Level 6 phrases are draft web-researched sentences and must pass native Moroccan Darija QA before audio recording or media production.
