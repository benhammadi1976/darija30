# D86 Level 9 Web Research Notes — Work & Practical Business

Scope: Level 9 teaches practical workplace and small-business communication in Morocco: introductions, appointments, WhatsApp/email follow-up, reception, client needs, meetings, quotation, invoice/payment, delivery, delays, negotiation, approvals, contracts, banking/accounting basics, coworking/printing, freelancers, customer service, networking, trade fairs, and admin paperwork.

Web-research anchors used before drafting:
- Darija business phrase references emphasize scheduling appointments, meeting vocabulary, negotiation, and polite commercial communication.
- Moroccan business etiquette references emphasize personal relationships, small talk, indirect communication, patience, hierarchy, and not rushing decisions.
- Practical Morocco business sources emphasize paperwork, invoices, quotes, payment proofs, and local communication habits.

D86 writing rules:
- Keep every sentence tied to one clear work/business scene.
- Use realistic Moroccan code-switching where common: rendez-vous, devis, facture, virement, TVA, contrat, manager, demo, email, WhatsApp.
- Avoid legal/financial advice; the learner only learns how to ask, confirm, and communicate professionally.
- All sentences are draft and must pass native Moroccan Darija QA before recording audio or creating media.
