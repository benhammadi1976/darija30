# D87 Level 10 Web Research Notes — Moroccan Culture Deep Dive

## Scope
Level 10 focuses on Moroccan culture beyond survival language: hospitality, tea, home visits, eating etiquette, Friday/family rhythms, Ramadan/Eid, hammam, weddings/henna, music, Amazigh diversity, medina/neighborhood respect, indirect communication, compliments, privacy/photos, dress/public-space respect, condolences, rural visits, crafts, environmental respect, and cultural-boundary repair phrases.

## Research signals used
- Moroccan hospitality and guest etiquette sources emphasize warm greetings, home invitations, thanking hosts, and farewell phrases such as `bslama`, `shukran 3la diyafa`, and `tji m3a lkhir`.
- Cultural etiquette sources emphasize mint tea as a hospitality symbol and recommend bringing a small gift such as sweets, fruit, or flowers when invited to a home.
- Moroccan festival/Ramadan sources emphasize Eid greetings such as `3id mubarak said` and family visits, and describe Ramadan/ftour as a major social rhythm.
- Moroccan phrase references confirm everyday phrases such as `shukran`, `afak/3afak`, `bismillah`, `inshallah`, `hamdullah`, `bssa7a`, and `marhba`.
