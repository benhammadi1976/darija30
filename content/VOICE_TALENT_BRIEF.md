# Darija30 Voice Talent Brief — Phase D12

## Goal
Record clear, natural Moroccan Darija audio for English-speaking beginners. The voice should sound like a helpful Moroccan friend, not like a classroom robot.

## Required voice
- Native Moroccan Darija speaker.
- Warm, clear, friendly tone.
- Neutral Moroccan pronunciation acceptable across Casablanca/Marrakech/Rabat style speech.
- No theatrical exaggeration.
- No music, background noise, reverb, or effects.

## Speeds
Each phrase needs two files:
1. **Normal**: natural speed, as a Moroccan would say it.
2. **Slow**: slower and clearer, but still natural. Do not split the phrase word-by-word like a machine.

## Recording format
- MP3.
- One phrase per file.
- File names must match `content/REAL_AUDIO_IMPORT_MANIFEST.csv` exactly.
- Keep a short clean silence at beginning/end, but no long gaps.

## Quality rule
The learner should be able to press Play once, repeat confidently, and understand the phrase from the lesson context.

## Do not record
- AI/robot voice for public launch.
- MSA pronunciation.
- Over-formal Arabic pronunciation.
- Different words than the Friendly Latin phrase unless intentionally approved.
